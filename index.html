❓ New to this? Registrations and calls, explained (30-second read)

When a desk phone, softphone app, or gateway boots up, it registers — it tells the phone system "I'm extension 20321, here's where you can find me." Think of it as checking in at a hotel front desk: until you check in, nobody can call your room. This file is a recording of that check-in conversation.

The words you'll see: the registrar is the front desk; REGISTER is the phone saying "check me in"; 200 OK is the desk saying "welcome"; a 401 is "what's the password?"; and Expires is how long the check-in lasts before the phone quietly renews it. NAT just means the phone sits behind a home/office router, so the server writes down the router's public address instead of the phone's private one.

Calls work the same way with different words: INVITE is "please connect me to this number", 180 Ringing is the ringback, 200 OK + ACK means the call connected, and BYE is a hang-up. One phone call often shows up as several legs inside the platform — the tool stitches those back into one call for you. These trace files carry the signaling only (the "phone system paperwork"), not the audio itself.
